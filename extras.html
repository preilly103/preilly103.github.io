Gobble Rockets

I joined Gobble Rockets at the beginning of my freshman year, looking for a design team to fill my interest in aerospace although I am a computer science major. Gobble Rockets is an all freshman design team, where we were challenged with making a rocket that could launch, land, and deploy a small rover to take a picture of the rocket. During this process I learned how to use CAD modeling software, various machining tools, 3D printing software Cura, and plenty more about aerospace and how rockets actually work. I made sure to help keep my team on track and work together, and we ended up placing 3rd overall at our competition. I also created and successfully launched a personal rocket in order to obtain my L1 High Powered Rocketry Certification.

Boy Scouts / Eagle Scout

Boy Scouts was a key part of my life for 8 years. I held numerous positions, each teaching me something new. Starting out as a Patrol Leader, I learned to organize and lead a small group of friends at campouts and meetings. I then became a Quartermaster, where I spent my meetings organizing gear and preparing for campouts to make sure all proper gear makes it to the site. I then became Senior Patrol Leader, where I learned to lead and plan meetings for 50 scouts, delegate tasks to middle leadership positions, and become the connection between the scouts and adults. During this time, I put all these skills together to create two in-ground chess tables and refurbish a shuffleboard court. This was all done for an affordable living community for senior citizens, and I attained the rank of Eagle.
